Research on Sim-to-Real in Reinforcement LearningApr. 2019 - Present Reinforcement learning is advancing in applications such as robot control, but training robots to interact in the real world can be costly and risky. A solution is to operate agents in the real world that were pre-trained in simulators, but accurate simulators are not always available. Especially for vision-based agents that receive camera images as state observations, it is generally difficult to equip simulators with high-precision renderers. The problem of bridging the gap between simulators and the real world in reinforcement learning is studied in the field known as Sim-to-Real. We propose a method for effectively utilizing agents pre-trained in simulators in the real world, even under challenging conditions with only low-precision renderers, or sometimes even without any renderers at all. The paper on this study was accepted at the IJCNN, with an acceptance rate of 55.4% (~ 1467/2646 for IEEE WCCI).
