Research on Sim-to-Real in Reinforcement LearningApr. 2019 - 2022, 2025
  • A common approach to applying reinforcement learning in real-world applications is to pretrain agents using simulators or offline datasets, as training them while interacting with the real world can be both costly and risky. However, since pre-trained agents may still fail in real-world operations, a method to bridge the gap between safe pre-training and actual deployment is necessary.
  • Led research proposing a method for pretraining vision-based agents in simulators without requiring a renderer; the paper was accepted at IJCNN with an acceptance rate of 55.4% (1467 out of 2646 for IEEE WCCI).

Research on Neural Architecture SearchApr. 2018 - 2022
  • Neural Architecture Search (NAS) algorithmically optimizes the architecture of deep neural networks, and gradient-based NAS defines differentiable architectures to simultaneously optimize both the architecture and weight parameters via gradient descent.
  • Led research on gradient-based NAS, proposing a method that converges with fewer updates while minimizing GPU memory usage; received the JSAI Annual Conference Student Incentive Award in Jul. 2019 and was accepted at AAAI with an acceptance rate of 21.4% (1692 out of 7911).
  • Contributed to research on automatic termination for gradient-based NAS by exploiting an upper bound on the decay of generalization error to determine when to stop the search process.
